# High-Bypass-Turbofan-Engine-Efficiency-Analysis
An independent aerospace engineering analysis behind the design ideology of modern jet propulsion systems, including its aerodynamic compatibility with the airframe it is attached to, its thermal efficiency, material choice and reasoning and the technological challenges accompanied with building a next generation engine to power modern aircrafts.

  Overview:
- Fundamentals of turboprop engines
- High-bypass ratio and propulsion efficiency
- Engine design
- Turbine thermodynamic efficiency and cooling implementation
- Materials used in production Aerodynamic properties to increase overall aircraft compatibility and efficiency
- Efficiency improvements over its history
- Environmental concerns and future improvements to limit negative impact
  
 Engines Studied:
 - GE Aerospace LEAP series
 - Rolls-Royce Trent XWB
 - Pratt & Whitney Geared Turbofan (GTF)
